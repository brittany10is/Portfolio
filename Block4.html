College Laundry System Redesign
This redesign was completed for the college course Design 1 (Design of Everyday Things). I worked with Roy Lincke, Vanessa Tse, Celine Wong, and EunSeo Cho on this project. Through user research, we were able to discover flaws in the on-campus laundry system at UCSD and purpose a solution to fulfill unmet user needs.

Roles

UX Research & Ideation

Duration

June 2017


OVERVIEW/DESIGN GOAL
The UCSD on-campus laundry system has many imperfections and has a lot of room for improvements and adjustments. Given the amount of complaints we received from our surveys and interviews, it is clear that a majority of the students who use the laundry machine has a general dissatisfaction with the system.
MIND MAP
Before going into observations and interviews, our group brainstormed the events that typically go on in an on-campus laundry room. We mentally went through our personal experiences and bounced ideas off of one another.
ONLINE SURVEY - 189 RESPONSES
Before conducting field work, our team decided to distribute a survey regarding the UCSD laundry systems in order to learn more about the user base before conducting hands-on research. The online survey that we distributed received 189 responses from anonymous UCSD students over the course of 9 days (5/18-5/26). In addition to asking them information about their status as a student (year, in which dorm they live, etc.), we collected valuable data regarding their thoughts on the current UCSD laundry system. From the bar graph below, it becomes evident that there is a large spread in student satisfaction for the laundry services on-campus which led us to believe that there was room for improvement.
FINAL REDESIGN
Our machine would have the user’s information from when they swiped their ID to pay for their load. We believe that this would reduce the number of students that forget to pick up their laundry allowing more washers and dryers to be available. Along with the pick up notification, students will have the opportunity to press a button indicating when a machine is broken. This system will allow for machines to be repaired faster and indicate to other students which machines are broken and should not be used.
The picture below shows the process that we are trying to implement in our redesign. The blue arrows indicate actions taken, the red is for the signifiers, and the yellow is for communication. Our redesign is not just a collection of tangible added features, but rather a whole process of interactions.
REDESIGN EXPLANATION
A lot of the problems, we found, stemmed from the fact that there was a lack of proper feedback from the system to the users. Such problems include people not picking up their laundry after it has finished, machines not giving proper notification that they do not work, a lack of enough laundry machines for the amount of users. Because of these reasons, we created a feedback system that gave proper notifications through text and/or email to the users, who would then be able to take the proper actions to minimize error. Although there are certainly trade-offs to our feedback redesign, we feel that the overall features would aid the users than without.
